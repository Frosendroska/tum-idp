--- throughput_timeline.png ---
Line plot, 30-second prorated windows. X-axis = wall clock time (06 20:10–21:25). Y-axis = Gbps.
Colour-coded by phase: red=warmup, blue=ramp_1, green=ramp_2, purple=ramp_3, orange=steady_state.

Observed behaviour (CRITICAL FINDING):
  1. Warmup + ramp phases (20:10–20:25): ~13.9–14.3 Gbps, stable, minor variance.
  2. Early steady_state (20:25–20:53): throughput holds at ≈14.2–14.3 Gbps — still in the
     high-performance regime despite concurrency dropping back to 96.
  3. Sudden cliff at ~20:53: throughput drops from 14.2 Gbps to ≈13.2 Gbps in one window, then
     continues falling sharply to ≈7.1 Gbps within the next 1–2 windows.
  4. Low-throughput steady regime (20:53–21:25): flat line at ≈7.1 Gbps with very low noise.
     A single late-run dip to ≈6.5 Gbps appears at the very end (~21:26).

The cliff at ≈20:53 is the dominant signal of this run. It represents an abrupt halving of
throughput (~14→7 Gbps) that persists for the remainder of the 60-minute steady-state window,
indicating the R2 backend imposed sustained-load throttling or a capacity ceiling after ≈28 min
of cumulative steady-state transfer.

=== CROSS-PLOT FINDINGS & KEY OBSERVATIONS ===

1. TWO-REGIME THROUGHPUT: The most important observation is the abrupt cliff at ≈20:53 (≈28 min
   into the 60-minute steady-state) where throughput halved from ≈14.3 to ≈7.1 Gbps.
   This coincides with a doubling of rolling-average latency. The transition is a step-function,
   not a gradual degradation, suggesting external throttling rather than internal resource
   exhaustion.

2. STABLE SECOND REGIME: After the cliff, throughput stabilised extremely flat at ≈7.1 Gbps for
   the remaining ≈32 minutes, suggesting the instance hit a hard bandwidth ceiling imposed by
   the R2 infrastructure.

3. RAMP PHASES UNAFFECTED: All ramp phases (warmup, ramp_1, ramp_2, ramp_3) sustained ≈14 Gbps
   regardless of concurrency level (48, 96, or 144). This suggests the throttling is
   time/volume-based rather than concurrency-based.

4. PERFECT RELIABILITY: 100% success rate, HTTP 206 throughout, only 6 trivial retries.
   The R2 service is highly reliable even under this sustained load.

5. CONCURRENCY EFFICIENCY: At 48 connections, each connection contributes ≈0.29 Gbps; at 96
   connections this drops to ≈0.11 Gbps (including throttled period). The marginal value of
   additional connections beyond 48 is sharply diminishing, especially post-cliff.

6. LATENCY CHARACTERISTICS: Even in the fastest regime (concurrency 48, pre-cliff), P99 latency
   is ≈5,549 ms, reflecting large object sizes being transferred (objects are ~96 MB each at
   ≈14 Gbps / 17 req/s). Absolute latency is dominated by transfer time, not connection overhead
   (RTT overhead is only ≈119–131 ms per request).
